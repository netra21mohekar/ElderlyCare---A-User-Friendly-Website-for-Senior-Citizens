Laws and Senior Citizen

Once an individual reaches the age of 60, they are designated as "Senior Citizens." Being labeled as a senior citizen implies that specific laws have been put in place for their benefit and protection. Unfortunately, many older adults are unaware of these regulations. In this article, we aim to provide insights into laws that are advantageous to the elderly. Recognizing the significant negligence faced by older adults, the government introduced the Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizen Act in 2007. The following are key provisions outlined in this act:

Security

This law is designed to ensure the safety and security of senior citizens, shielding them from potential harm caused by individuals or circumstances. In the event of any trouble faced by senior citizens due to another individual, they have the right to file a complaint directly with the District Magistrate's (DM) office or the nearest police station. A dedicated helpline number, 1291, has been established for senior citizens to register their complaints. As per this law, a senior citizen must file a complaint within 24 hours of the incident, and the DM is required to resolve the case within 30 days from the date of filing the complaint.

Care

Care: If a senior citizen is living with their family or independently and is unable to sustain themselves, the law permits them to request their children or close relatives to provide them with Rs 10,000 per month for their livelihood. In case the legal heir of the senior citizen refuses to provide subsistence, they will be liable to pay a fine of Rs 5000 or face a jail term of up to three months, or both.

Property

Senior citizens who own property cannot be compelled to vacate their premises. If such coercion occurs, they have the right to file a case against the perpetrators at the DM office. They are authorized to designate a legal heir for their property based on the care they receive. Free Legal Assistance

Senior citizens with an annual income of less than two lakhs are entitled to receive free legal assistance for any court-related matters.
